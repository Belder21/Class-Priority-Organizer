How Students Learn Best — Research Summary
Active Recall
Testing yourself without notes is far more effective than re-reading. Flashcards, practice problems, and blank-page summaries all count.
Spaced Repetition
Reviewing material at increasing intervals (1 day, 3 days, 1 week) dramatically improves long-term retention versus cramming.
Interleaved Practice
Alternating between subjects or problem types is harder but produces better learning than blocking one topic for hours.
Pomodoro Method
25-minute focused work sessions followed by 5-minute breaks improve concentration and prevent mental fatigue.
Sleep (8–10 hrs)
Memory consolidation happens during sleep. Teens need 8–10 hours. Studying then sleeping is better than pulling an all-nighter.
Movement Breaks
Short exercise breaks boost attention, mood, and memory. Even a 10-minute walk between sessions makes a measurable difference.
